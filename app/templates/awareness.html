Understanding the Disease

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer occurs when cells in the breast grow uncontrollably, forming a tumor that can often be felt as a lump or detected on an X-ray. It is the most commonly diagnosed cancer among women worldwide, though it can also affect men.

Breast cancer can begin in different parts of the breast — the ducts (ductal carcinoma), the lobules (lobular carcinoma), or, less frequently, in the tissue between them. It is classified into two main types:

Benign

Non-cancerous tumors that do not invade nearby tissue or spread to other parts of the body.

Malignant

Cancerous tumors that can invade nearby tissue and spread to other parts of the body (metastasize).

Types of Breast Cancer
01
Invasive Ductal Carcinoma (IDC)
Most common — starts in milk ducts, spreads to surrounding tissue.
02
Invasive Lobular Carcinoma (ILC)
Second most common — starts in milk-producing lobules.
03
Triple-Negative Breast Cancer
Aggressive form that lacks three common receptors; harder to treat.
04
HER2-Positive Breast Cancer
Produces excess HER2 protein; tends to grow faster but responds well to targeted therapy.
05
Inflammatory Breast Cancer (IBC)
Rare, aggressive — lymph vessels in breast skin are blocked by cancer cells.
Know Your Risk

Risk Factors for Breast Cancer

Understanding risk factors helps in making informed decisions about screening and lifestyle changes.

Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

Factors you cannot change:

  • Being female (women are 100× more at risk than men)
  • Increasing age (risk rises after 50)
  • Family history of breast or ovarian cancer
  • Inherited BRCA1 / BRCA2 gene mutations
  • Dense breast tissue
  • Personal history of breast conditions
Modifiable Risk Factors

Factors you may be able to change:

  • Being overweight or obese (especially after menopause)
  • Physical inactivity
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Hormone replacement therapy (HRT)
  • Smoking
  • Exposure to radiation on chest
Protective Factors

Factors that may reduce your risk:

  • Regular physical exercise (150 min/week)
  • Maintaining a healthy body weight
  • Breastfeeding (if possible)
  • Limiting alcohol intake
  • Healthy diet rich in fruits & vegetables
  • Regular breast screening & mammograms
